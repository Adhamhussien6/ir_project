Climate Change and Global Warming

Climate change refers to long-term shifts in global temperatures and weather patterns. While climate change can occur naturally, since the industrial revolution human activities have been the main driver of climate change, primarily due to the burning of fossil fuels.

Burning coal, oil, and natural gas releases carbon dioxide and other greenhouse gases into the atmosphere. These gases trap heat from the sun, causing the Earth's temperature to rise. This phenomenon is known as the greenhouse effect and is the primary cause of global warming.

The effects of climate change are widespread and severe. Rising sea levels threaten coastal cities and island nations. Extreme weather events such as hurricanes, droughts, and floods are becoming more frequent and intense. Arctic ice is melting at an unprecedented rate, disrupting ecosystems and affecting weather patterns worldwide.

Biodiversity is under threat as species struggle to adapt to rapidly changing conditions. Coral reefs, often called the rainforests of the sea, are bleaching and dying due to warmer and more acidic oceans. Agriculture is being disrupted, threatening food security for millions of people.

To combat climate change, countries around the world are transitioning to renewable energy sources such as solar and wind power. International agreements like the Paris Agreement aim to limit global temperature rise to 1.5 degrees Celsius above pre-industrial levels.

Individual actions also matter. Reducing energy consumption, eating less meat, using public transportation, and supporting sustainable businesses all contribute to reducing carbon emissions. Collective action at every level of society is essential to address this global challenge.
